Correlation

The correlation is a quantitative measure to describes coherences. The correlation analysis considers criterias (e.g. experimental results) and measures - based on a hypothesis - their connection properties.

The quality of the correspondence between criterias and the considered hypothesis is characterised by the correlation coefficient.

The correlation coefficient r is a number of the range -1 and +1. In the case r is near by +1 (r is near by -1) then there is a good connection between the criterias and the hyptothesis (between the criterias and and the "reverse" hypothesis).

If r is near by 0 it is suggested that the criterias are "non correlated" to the hypothesis.

The statistical cryptanalysis is searching for a hypothesis that has a maximum correlation.
