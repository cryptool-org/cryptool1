Use of encryption algorithms at the Deutsche Bank

Cryptographic algorithms and protocols form the basis for many different security mechanisms and security services, such as, for example,

protection of the confidentiality of documents,

proof of the origin of messages,

user authentication (authenticity),

assurance of the binding nature of transactions (non-repudiation),

assurance of the integrity of data.

These security mechanisms and services are used in many different applications, for which they have to be implemented on many different system platforms, from smart cards through to mainframe computers.

At the Deutsche Bank cryptographic algorithms are used in some very different areas. For example,

Internally:

to protect e-mails and file transfers

for remote access

to encrypt hard disks on PCs

To protect customer applications:

Internet banking and Internet broking

home banking (HBCI)

electronic banking (BCS)

smart card applications (money card (GeldKarte), ec card with chip)

electronic money (e-cash)

e-commerce (SET transactions)

For applications with multiple banks involved:

electronic settlement with the Federal Bank (Bundesbank)

SWIFT

The Triple-DES algorithm is the standard encryption algorithm used at the Deutsche Bank, while the RSA algorithm is standard for digital signatures. RIPEMD-160 is used as the standard hash function.

As well as the specific security requirements of different applications, there are often special requirements and/or restrictions relating to the use of encryption algorithms, so that additional procedures are used as well.
