Dialogue Generate Prime Numbers (demonstration)

Prime numbers are natural numbers greater than 1, which are divisible only by 1 and by themselves, and in cryptography they play an important role. In this demonstration you can generate prime numbers yourself, the prime numbers being generated randomly within freely definable limits.

If you press the Generate prime numbers button, a random search for prime numbers for p and q will be initiated. A number X will be taken at a random from the range specified [Lower limit ... Upper limit]. The system then "tests" for X whether X can be a prime number. Once again, chance is used for this test:

If X is not a prime number, then the chosen test states with a probability of ½ that X is not a prime number, whereas in the other case the message is always positive. If the prime number test is repeated 80 times "independently" and always has a positive outcome, then the probability that X is not actually a prime number is extremely small at 2^(-80) and the number thus found is interpreted as a prime number and is output as either prime number p or prime number q.

If one of k prime number test is negative, then X is not prime and the test is repeated with a new randomly chosen number X.

In this dialogue you can choose between the following prime number tests:

  • Miller-Rabin Test,

  • Solovay-Strassen Test and

  • Fermat Test

If no prime number has been found after trying 1000 randomly drawn numbers X, then an appropriate error message is displayed. In that case you need to raise the permitted value limits [Lower limit ... Upper limit] for the prime numbers accordingly.

Detailed information on the subject of prime numbers will be found in the script.
