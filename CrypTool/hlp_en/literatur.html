Literature

The sources listed here help deepening your knowledge – they are by no means comprehensive.

A number of sources were used during the development of CrypTool.

The online help available within CrypTool exhaustively explains the classic encryption algorithms. Mostly the online help doesn't go deeply into the modern encryption algorithms due to their complexity.

Further information and further sources concerning also the modern cryptographic algorithms can be found within single articles of the CrypTool script and within the "Links" section on the CrypTool web site.

The following lists of references are alphabetically sorted.

Links to the Enigma machine

The Word War II Enigma machine is addressed in an especially large number of references and versions on the Internet.

  • Codes and Ciphers in the Second World War: The Enigma
    offers an explanation of how the machine worked, as well as an introduction to substitution ciphers
    http://www.codesandciphers.org.uk/enigma/index.htm
  • Cryptography: Enigma Cipher
    outlines the workings of the Enigma cipher
    http://www.trincoll.edu/depts/cpsc/cryptography/enigma.html
  • Enigma and the Codebreakers
    online exhibit from the Imperial War Museum
    http://www.iwm.org.uk/online/enigma/eni-intro.htm
  • Enigma and the Turing Bombe
    article examining the Enigma machine and the theory, construction, and method of the Turing Bombe.
    http://www.geocities.com/CapeCanaveral/Hangar/4040/bombe.html
  • Enigma Machine background and links from David Hamer‚s Web Site
    http://www.eclipse.net/~dhamer/Enigma1.htm
  • German Enigma Cipher Machine
    presents a history of the work to break the Enigma code
    http://www.enigmahistory.org
  • Myke Predko‚s Enigma Code/Decode Page
    includes history and an Enigma simulator (source code and program)
    http://www.myke.com/enigma.htm
  • Flash simulation of the three-rotor Enigma by Frank Spiess (used in CrypTool)
    http://enigmaco.de/index-enigma.html
