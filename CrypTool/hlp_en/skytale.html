Scytale of Sparta (500 ad) (described by Plutarch)

Sender and receiver both need 2 cylinders (squared timbers) of the

same radius and of the same number of corners.

The sender winds a small ribbon of vellum spirally round his cylinder and writes lengthwise his message onto the ribbon. The message on the unwinded ribbon can be read only by a person who has a cylinder of the same kind.

How can this algorithm be modelled within CrypTool ?

The Skytale is equivalent to the identical permutation (1,2,3, ..., n) with n columns (n was the number of corners of the Skytale).

Example:

For n=3 (exactly 3 corners) the sender writes onto the ribbon:

JAM ESB OND

and gets an unwinded ribbon with:

JEO ASN MBD.

If he writes 

    JAM ESB OND
    WAN TED DEA
    DOR ALI VE

he gets 

    JEO WTD DAV
    ASN AEE OLE
    MBD NDA RI
You can directly call this cipher here.
