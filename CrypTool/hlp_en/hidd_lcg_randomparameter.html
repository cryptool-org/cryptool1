Dialog Linear Congruence Generator

The Linear Congruence Generator (LCG) is an algorithm for generating a sequence of apparently random whole numbers (X_[i]) in accordance with the recursive calculation rule:

X_[0] := secret seed value

X_[i+1] := a*X_[i] +b (mod N) for i = 0, 1, 2, 3, ...

whereby X_[i] are whole numbers from the range 0,1,2, ..., N-1. It is customary to standardize the random numbers to the rational value range [0,1[, hence the output as rational numbers Z[i] := X_[i]/N.

In CrypTool we observe only "coin throws" ("heads" are mapped to the 0 and "tails" to the 1). In the LCG the coin throw 0 is therefore represented by the event [Z_[i] < 0.5] and the coin throw 1 by the event [0.5 <= Z_[i] < 1]. The sequence of coin throws is saved as a binary file (a sequence of 8 coin throws produces one random ASCII character).

The parameters in LCG are the numbers N, a, b from the linear equation y = a*x+b (mod N): modulus N, the multiplier a: 0<a<N and the additive increment b: 1<b<N. Moreover, the LCG is initialized through the secret initial value or seed X_0.

Select Individual Procedures \ Generate Random Numbers and activate the radio button Linear Congruence Generator (ICG). You can now specify the LCG parameters under "Choose generator-specific parameters...".

Please note that the quality of the LCG depends critically on the choice of parameters. An important dimension here is the period length of the random sequence, which is determined by the parameters.

LCG is widely known as its simple structure makes it implementable and permits high-performance. A large proportion of the pseudorandom number functions in operating systems (e.g. UNIX) and programming languages (such as ANSI C, Fortran etc.) are implemented with LCG. Due to its popularity, LCG is one of the best and most analyzed random number generators.
