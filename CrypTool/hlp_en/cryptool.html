The CrypTool Project

The CrypTool project develops the world's most-widespread e-learning programs (educational tools) in the area of cryptography and cryptanalysis.

It consists (status July 2022) of six different subprojects – four of these subprojects (CT1, CT2, JCT, CTO) are related to the CrypTool software (tools) in various facets for different purposes:

  • CrypTool 1 (CT1) was the first version of CrypTool. It was released in 1998 and allows to experiment with different cryptographic algorithms. CT1 has the two desktop successors CT2 and JCT, and the browser successor CTO.

  • CrypTool 2 (CT2) supports visual programming and execution of cascades of cryptographic procedures. It is a very rich .NET program. The workspaces inside can be zoomed.

  • The platform-independent JCrypTool (JCT) allows to give cryptography and cryptanalysis a chance on different operating systems (Windows, macOS, Linux). It is an Eclipse program.

  • CrypTool-Online (CTO) was first released in spring 2009. This tool allows to try out different algorithms in a browser and so also on a smartphone.

The remaining two subprojects are:

  • The international cipher contestMysteryTwister (MTC3) offering many cryptographic riddles ("challenges") of different levels.

  • The CrypTool Book (CTB) explains cryptography with a bit more mathematics than the tools andit concretely invites you to try it out with different tools.
CrypTool raises awareness and interest in cryptography for everyone.
All learning programs in the CrypTool project are open source and available for free.

Volunteers, especially programmers and students planning to write their thesis in the area of cryptography, are always welcome to join in the further development of the CrypTool project.
