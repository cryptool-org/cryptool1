Period length of a pseudorandom number generator

Period p of a pseudorandom number generator is determined by the interval at which a pseudorandom number in the generated output sequence X_[i] is repeated for the first time, that is, for every i: X_[i] = X_[i+p]. The period is dependent on the skilful choice of parameters for the generator, as only with a suitable choice of parameters can the generator achieve the biggest possible period.

Once the periodicity is known, the random number generator is cracked, as for every pseudorandom number X_[i] the next number X[i+1] is known. Good pseudorandom number generators therefore have very large period lengths where in practice it is not possible to save all the sequence members of the period.
