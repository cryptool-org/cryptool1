Crypto competitions / Crypto puzzles

There are many cryptographic challenges and puzzles published. Here are some more well know.

1) Schmeh's challenges

Attached within CT1 are the following 13 encrypted text files which Klaus Schmeh compiled in his book "Codebreaker vs. Codemaker" [1].

These texts contain some historically interesting (solved and unsolved) ciphertexts one can very well use as practice material and entertainment:

  1. Dorabella cipher (unsolved)
    Schmeh01-Dorabella-enc.txt
  2. D’Agapeyeff cipher (unsolved)
    Schmeh02-Agapeyeff-enc.txt
  3. Feynman cipher (3 parts, of which two are unsolved)
    Schmeh03a-Feynman-enc.txt
    Schmeh03b-Feynman-enc.txt
    Schmeh03c-Feynman-enc.txt
  4. Kryptos sculpture (4 parts, of which the last one is unsolved)
    Schmeh04a-Kryptos-enc.txt
    Schmeh04b-Kryptos-enc.txt
    Schmeh04c-Kryptos-enc.txt
    Schmeh04d-Kryptos-enc.txt
  5. Double-column transposition (created by Klaus Schmeh, proposed by Otto Leiberich, plaintext is English, unsolved)
    Schmeh05-Doppelwuerfel-enc.txt
  6. "Die tanzenden Maennchen" by Arthur Conan Doyle (Sherlock Holmes), (transcribed, solved)
    Schmeh06-SherlockHolmes-enc.txt
  7. Harry Potter cipher (created by Klaus Schmeh, plaintext is German, unsolved)
    Schmeh07-HarryPotter-enc.txt
  8. "Mathias Sandorf" by Jules Verne (solved)
    Schmeh08-MathiasSandorf-enc.txt

Particularly exciting is number 5, the double-column transposition.

If used correctly, this historic cipher even resists modern attacks because it holds roughly the same resistibility (key space) as RC5.

To make this comprehensible, Mr. Schmeh launched a cryptanalysis competition. The exercise is based on an idea by Otto Leiberich, former president of the German Information Security Agency (BSI). Leiberich reported on the double-column transposition in a magazine article in 1999 [2].

The double-column transposition is an encryption algorithm that can be executed by hand without the help of a computer and is still considered quite safe. The double-column transposition was (and probably still is) often used by spies who did not want to arouse suspicion by using an encryption device or a computer program.

In spite of its wide usage, only a few scientific analyses are known. Thus, the security of the algorithm can hardly be estimated. For this reason, Otto Leibereich created a crypto challenge in his article. Klaus Schmeh encrypted an English text with two passwords of different length and published the result in his book "Codebreaker vs. Codemaker" [1] and in a Telepolis article [3].

Everybody who wants to intensively deal with this challenge is encouraged to approach the CrypTool team.

2) Further cipher challenges / contests (mostly mathematically oriented)

  • From Simon Singh's "The Code Book": solved,
    see http://www.simonsingh.net/Crypto_Corner.html
  • RSA cipher or RSA factoring challenges (started in 1991, ended in 2007): This is the most well-known cipher contest. For some of the challenges prize money was advertised for the decryption.
    See http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/RSA_Factoring_Challenge
  • RSA Secret-Key Challenge
    see http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/RSA_Secret-Key_Challenge
  • Certicom (RIM) ECC Challenge 1997
    see http://www.certicom.com/ and https://www.certicom.com/index.php/the-certicom-ecc-challenge
  • Enigma Cipher Challenge
    see http://enigma.maths.org/project/ecc.php
  • Applied Crypto Challenge Problems (Mark Stamp)
    see http://cs.sjsu.edu/faculty/stamp/crypto/ChallengeProblems/challengeProblems.html
  • Number Shark or Taxman Challenge
    This teaching game was implemented in CT1 and JCT. Additionally the authors investigated a bit the mathematical borders for finding the optimal integer sequence http://www.research.att.com/~njas/sequences/A019312.
  • TU Darmstadt Lattice Challenge 2008
    see http://www.latticechallenge.org
  • Mystery Twister started in 2005 and was a large-scale contest of the university of Bochum. It could not be finished due to the passing of Hans Dobbertin and missing sponsors. This challenge was revived from 2010 in a new form together with the CrypTool team. Since then it is one of the most active and diversified contests of this kind: MysteryTwister C3.
